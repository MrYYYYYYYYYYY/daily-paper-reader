## 摘要
本文研究自然语言广义量词的可定义性。广义量词的语义由论域子集的集合提供。然而，自然语言中出现的广义量词要么通过一阶量化定义，要么通过基数概念定义。本文为这一观察到的现象提供了解释。解释是，当域独立性和保守性这两个著名约束扩展到Henkin模型时，足以确保低层次的可定义性，即$\Delta^1_1$-可定义性或至少$\Sigma^1_1$-可定义性；并且在大多数情况下，这种可定义性可以是有界的。这基本上是Feferman保持定理的结果，Marker为其提供了一个简短的模型论证明。此外，我们验证了对于合理的背景理论选择，典型的基数量词确实是$\Delta^1_1$-可定义的。最后，在许多其他情况下，我们证明这种可定义性可以降低到一阶可定义性。

## Abstract
This paper studies the definability of natural language generalized quantifiers. The semantics of generalized quantifiers are provided by a collection of subsets of the underlying domain. However, the generalized quantifiers appearing in natural language are definable either by first-order quantification or by cardinality notions. This paper provides an explanation for this observed phenomenon. The explanation is that the famous constraints of domain independence and conservativity, when extended to Henkin models, suffice to ensure low-level definability, namely $Δ^1_1$-definability or at least $Σ^1_1$-definability; and in most cases this definability can be made to be bounded. This is basically a consequence of Feferman's Preservation Theorem, which Marker has provided a short model-theoretic proof of. Further, we verify that the paradigmatic cardinality quantifiers are indeed $Δ^1_1$-definable for a reasonable choice of background theory. Finally, in many other cases, we show that this definability can be lowered to first-order definability.
